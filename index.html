Facebook Data Breach

Posted on January 1, 2021
Facebook Social Media

A massive data breach has occurred at Facebook compromising the information of over 500 millin people. If you use Facebook, be wary of potential incoming phishing attempts from any sources. Although there was no highly confidential information leaked such as social security numbers or credit card numbers, the data that was leaked could include: Facebook ID, full name, location, birthday, and most importantly: phone number and email.

This breach affects people from over 100 countries including 32 million individuals data from the USA, 11 million from the UK, and 6 million from India.

How it happened.

The data breach is believed to relate to a vulnerability which Facebook reportedly fixed in August of 2019. While the exact source of the data canâ€™t be verified, it was likely acquired through the misuse of legitimate functions in the Facebook systems. Such misuses can occur when a seemingly innocent feature of a website is used for an unexpected purpose by attackers, as was the case with a PayID attack in 2019. This breach occured when attackers abused a flaw in a Facebook address book contacts import feature. Facebook says it patched the vulnerability in August 2019, but it's unclear how many times the bug was exploited before then. The information from more than 500 million Facebook users in more than 106 countries contains Facebook IDs, phone numbers, and other information about early Facebook users like Mark Zuckerburg and US secretary of Transportation Pete Buttigieg, as well as the European Union commissioner for data protection, Didier Reynders. Other victims include 61 people who list the "Federal Trade Commission" and 651 people who list "Attorney General" in their details on Facebook.

OneClass Data Breach

Posted on June 29, 2020
OneClass Education

If you're a North American student who uses OneClass, you may not look as happy as the students pictured above. OneClass is an educational service for post secondary education, and is used to consoldate notes, class materials, and things of that nature.

Your information was left on an unsecured, public cloud for anyone to access. Information leaked includes full names, email addresses, account details (such as your password) and phone numbers. We reccommend that everyone update their passwords that you share with OneClass, as well as adding 2 factor authentication if you haven't already.
